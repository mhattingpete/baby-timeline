## Overview

The umbilical cord stump (navlestumpen) is what remains after the cord is cut at birth. It requires simple care and will fall off naturally within 1-3 weeks.

> **Danish:** "En navlestump kræver som regel ingen særlig pleje men skal have luft og holdes tør og ren."
>
> **English:** "An umbilical stump usually requires no special care but should have air and be kept dry and clean."
>
> *Source: sundhed.dk*

---

## Timeline

| Stage | Timeframe | Description |
|-------|-----------|-------------|
| At birth | Day 0 | Cord clamped and cut, 2-3 cm stump remains |
| Drying | Days 1-7 | Stump dries out, becomes darker |
| Separation | Days 7-21 | Stump naturally separates and falls off |
| Healing | 1-2 weeks after | Small wound heals completely |

> **Danish:** "Navlestumpen falder normalt af efter 7-10 dage, men det kan tage op til 3 uger."
>
> **English:** "The umbilical stump usually falls off after 7-10 days, but it can take up to 3 weeks."

## Care Instructions (Plejeinstruktioner)

### What TO Do

| Action | Danish | Details |
|--------|--------|---------|
| Keep it dry | Hold den tør | Pat dry after baths |
| Keep it clean | Hold den ren | Clean with water if dirty |
| Give it air | Giv den luft | Fold diaper below stump |
| Let it fall naturally | Lad den falde naturligt | Don't pull or twist |
| Wash hands | Vask hænder | Before touching the area |

### What NOT To Do

| Don't | Danish | Why |
|-------|--------|-----|
| Don't use alcohol/disinfectant | Brug ikke sprit/desinfektionsmiddel | May delay healing |
| Don't cover with bandages | Dæk ikke med forbinding | Needs air |
| Don't pull it off | Træk den ikke af | Can cause bleeding/infection |
| Don't submerge in bath | Nedsænk ikke i bad | Keep dry until healed |
| Don't apply lotions/powders | Påfør ikke lotion/pudder | Can cause irritation |

> **Danish:** "Der anbefales ikke længere at anvende sprit eller desinfektionsmiddel på navlestumpen."
>
> **English:** "Alcohol or disinfectant is no longer recommended for use on the umbilical stump."

## Diaper Positioning (Bleplacering)

To keep the stump dry and exposed to air:

| Technique | Danish | How |
|-----------|--------|-----|
| Fold down front | Fold fronten ned | Roll or fold top of diaper below stump |
| Use newborn diapers | Brug nyfødt bleer | Some have cutout for cord |
| Check after changes | Tjek efter skift | Ensure stump is visible |

---

## Normal Changes (Normale Ændringer)

| Change | Description | Concern Level |
|--------|-------------|---------------|
| Color darkens | Changes from yellowish to brown/black | Normal |
| Dries out | Becomes hard and shriveled | Normal |
| Slight odor | Mild smell as it dries | Normal |
| Small amount of bleeding | When separating | Normal |
| Clear/yellow discharge | Small amount | Usually normal |

### What Falling Off Looks Like

1. Stump darkens and shrivels
2. Base becomes loose
3. Hangs by a thread
4. Falls off (may leave small raw area)
5. Area heals within 1-2 weeks

---

## Bathing Before Cord Falls Off (Badning Før Navlen Falder Af)

| Type | Recommendation | Details |
|------|----------------|---------|
| Sponge bath | Recommended | Keep cord dry |
| Tub bath | Wait until cord falls off | Or keep stump above water |
| After bath | Pat dry thoroughly | Ensure area is completely dry |

> **Danish:** "Du kan give dit barn et bad, men sørg for at navlestumpen tørrer godt bagefter."
>
> **English:** "You can bathe your baby, but make sure the umbilical stump dries well afterwards."

## Signs of Infection (Tegn på Infektion)

### Contact Healthcare Provider If:

| Sign | Danish | Description |
|------|--------|-------------|
| Redness spreading | Rødme der spreder sig | Around the base, expanding |
| Swelling | Hævelse | Area around stump |
| Foul smell | Ildelugtende | Strong, bad odor |
| Pus/discharge | Pus/væske | Yellow or green discharge |
| Bleeding | Blødning | More than a few drops |
| Baby seems unwell | Baby virker utilpas | Fever, lethargy, poor feeding |
| Hasn't fallen off by 4 weeks | Ikke faldet af efter 4 uger | May need evaluation |

> **Danish:** "Kontakt læge hvis navlen er rød, hævet, ildelugtende, eller hvis der kommer pus."
>
> **English:** "Contact a doctor if the navel is red, swollen, smelly, or if pus is coming out."

### Omphalitis (Navlebetændelse)

Omphalitis is an infection of the umbilical stump that requires medical treatment.

| Symptom | Danish |
|---------|--------|
| Spreading redness | Spredende rødme |
| Warmth around area | Varme omkring området |
| Discharge with odor | Væske med lugt |
| Baby may have fever | Baby kan have feber |

**Seek medical attention promptly if you suspect infection.**

## After the Cord Falls Off

### Normal Healing

| Observation | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Small raw area | Normal, will heal |
| Slight bleeding | Normal for a day or two |
| Crusty appearance | Normal during healing |
| Complete healing | Within 1-2 weeks |

### Care After Separation

| Action | Details |
|--------|---------|
| Keep clean | Gentle cleaning with water |
| Keep dry | Pat dry, give air |
| Watch for infection | Same warning signs |
| Can bathe normally | Once healed (usually 1-2 days) |

---

## Umbilical Granuloma (Navlegranulom)

Sometimes a small pink/red growth appears after the cord falls off.

| Feature | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Appearance | Small, pink/red, moist lump |
| Location | In or around navel |
| Concern level | Low - usually harmless |
| Treatment | May need silver nitrate treatment by doctor |

> **Danish:** "Et navlegranulom er en lille rød vækst, der kan dannes i navlen. Det er ikke farligt og kan behandles af lægen."
>
> **English:** "An umbilical granuloma is a small red growth that can form in the navel. It is not dangerous and can be treated by the doctor."

---

## Umbilical Hernia (Navlebrok)

A small bulge at the navel when baby cries or strains.

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Appearance | Bulge at navel, especially when crying |
| Cause | Gap in abdominal muscles |
| Concern level | Usually low |
| Treatment | Most close on their own by age 1-2 |
| When to see doctor | If larger than 2 cm, not improving, or causes pain |

> **Danish:** "Navlebrok er meget almindeligt og forsvinder som regel af sig selv inden barnet er 2 år."
>
> **English:** "Umbilical hernia is very common and usually resolves on its own before the child is 2 years old."

## Key Messages for Parents

1. **Keep it simple** - Clean, dry, and exposed to air
2. **No special products needed** - Water is enough
3. **Let it fall naturally** - Don't pull or twist
4. **Fold the diaper** - Keep it below the stump
5. **Know infection signs** - Redness, swelling, odor, discharge
6. **Normal timeline** - 7-21 days, but up to 4 weeks can be normal
7. **When in doubt, ask** - Contact sundhedsplejerske or doctor
