## Overview

Jaundice (gulsot) is very common in newborns, affecting approximately 60% of full-term babies and 80% of premature babies. It is usually harmless and resolves on its own.

> **Danish:** "I vore dage er gulsot ikke farligt, hvis det bliver opdaget og behandlet i tide."
>
> **English:** "Nowadays, jaundice is not dangerous if it is detected and treated in time."
>
> *Source: sundhed.dk*

---

## What is Jaundice?

Jaundice is a yellowing of the skin and whites of the eyes caused by a buildup of bilirubin in the blood. Bilirubin is a yellow substance produced when red blood cells break down.

| Danish Term | English Term | Description |
|-------------|--------------|-------------|
| Gulsot | Jaundice | Yellow discoloration of skin/eyes |
| Bilirubin | Bilirubin | Yellow pigment from red blood cell breakdown |
| Fysiologisk gulsot | Physiological jaundice | Normal newborn jaundice |
| Patologisk gulsot | Pathological jaundice | Abnormal jaundice requiring treatment |

---

## Timeline

### Normal Physiological Jaundice

| Timeframe | What Happens |
|-----------|--------------|
| Day 2-4 | Onset - jaundice typically appears |
| Day 3-5 | Peak - highest bilirubin levels |
| Day 7-14 | Resolution - usually resolves |
| Up to 3 weeks | Breastfed babies may take longer to resolve |

> **Danish:** "Fysiologisk gulsot opstår typisk på 2.-4. levedøgn og forsvinder i løbet af 1-2 uger."
>
> **English:** "Physiological jaundice typically occurs on days 2-4 of life and disappears within 1-2 weeks."
>
> *Source: sundhed.dk*

### When Jaundice is Concerning

> **Danish:** "Gulsot som starter sent eller strækker sig udover 14. dagen skal udredes."
>
> **English:** "Jaundice that starts late or extends beyond day 14 should be investigated."
>
> *Source: sundhed.dk*

| Timing | Concern Level | Action |
|--------|---------------|--------|
| Within 24 hours of birth | High | Requires immediate evaluation |
| Day 2-4 | Normal | Monitor, usually physiological |
| Beyond 14 days | Moderate | Should be evaluated |
| Beyond 21 days | High | Requires investigation |

## Risk Factors (Risikofaktorer)

| Risk Factor | Danish | Notes |
|-------------|--------|-------|
| Premature birth | For tidlig fødsel | Immature liver function |
| Bruising during birth | Blødning under fødsel | More bilirubin from breakdown |
| Blood type incompatibility | Blodtypeuforenelighed | ABO or Rh incompatibility |
| Breastfeeding difficulties | Ammevanskeligheder | Inadequate milk intake |
| Sibling had jaundice | Søskende havde gulsot | Genetic factors |
| East Asian ancestry | Østasiatisk herkomst | Higher incidence |

## Symptoms (Symptomer)

### Visible Signs

| Sign | Danish | How to Check |
|------|--------|--------------|
| Yellow skin | Gul hud | Press lightly on forehead or nose |
| Yellow eye whites | Gule øjenhvider | Check in natural daylight |
| Yellow palms/soles | Gule håndflader/fodsåler | Indicates more severe jaundice |
| Dark urine | Mørk urin | Should be pale/colorless in newborns |
| Pale stools | Bleg afføring | Warning sign - see doctor |

### Progression of Yellowing

Jaundice typically progresses from head to toe as bilirubin levels increase:

| Bilirubin Level | Visible Jaundice |
|-----------------|------------------|
| Low | Face only |
| Moderate | Face, chest, abdomen |
| High | Arms, legs |
| Very high | Palms, soles |

---

## Warning Signs (Advarselstegn)

### Seek Immediate Medical Attention If:

| Sign | Danish | Why It's Serious |
|------|--------|------------------|
| Jaundice within 24 hours | Gulsot inden for 24 timer | May indicate blood incompatibility |
| Very yellow/orange skin | Meget gul/orange hud | High bilirubin levels |
| Baby very sleepy, hard to wake | Baby meget søvnig, svær at vække | May indicate severe jaundice |
| Poor feeding, weak suck | Dårlig amning, svagt sug | Not getting enough nutrition |
| High-pitched cry | Skingrende gråd | Neurological symptom |
| Arched back | Buet ryg | Neurological symptom |
| Fever | Feber | May indicate infection |
| Pale or white stools | Bleg eller hvid afføring | May indicate liver problem |

> **Danish:** "Kontakt læge straks hvis barnet er meget sløvt, ikke vil spise, har skingrende gråd, eller har bleg afføring."
>
> **English:** "Contact doctor immediately if the baby is very lethargic, won't eat, has a high-pitched cry, or has pale stools."

## Treatment (Behandling)

### Mild Jaundice (No Treatment Needed)

- Frequent feeding (8-12 times per day)
- Adequate hydration
- Monitor progression
- Natural daylight exposure (not direct sunlight)

### Moderate to Severe Jaundice

| Treatment | Danish | Description |
|-----------|--------|-------------|
| Phototherapy | Lysbehandling | Blue light breaks down bilirubin |
| Feeding support | Ammestøtte | Ensure adequate intake |
| IV fluids | Intravenøs væske | If dehydrated |
| Exchange transfusion | Udskiftningstransfusion | Severe cases only |

> **Danish:** "Lysbehandling er den mest almindelige behandling for gulsot. Det blå lys hjælper med at nedbryde bilirubin."
>
> **English:** "Phototherapy is the most common treatment for jaundice. The blue light helps break down bilirubin."

## Breastfeeding and Jaundice

### Breastfeeding Jaundice (Ammegulsot)

Occurs in first week when baby isn't getting enough breast milk.

| Solution | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| More frequent feeding | Feed 10-12 times per day |
| Ensure good latch | Seek lactation support |
| Monitor wet diapers | Should have 6+ per day by day 5 |
| Supplementation if needed | Under healthcare guidance |

### Breast Milk Jaundice (Modermælksgulsot)

Harmless jaundice that can last 2-3 months in exclusively breastfed babies.

> **Danish:** "Modermælksgulsot er ufarligt og kræver ikke at man stopper amningen."
>
> **English:** "Breast milk jaundice is harmless and does not require stopping breastfeeding."

| Characteristic | Details |
|----------------|---------|
| Onset | After first week |
| Duration | Can last 2-12 weeks |
| Treatment | None needed, continue breastfeeding |
| Concern level | Low - harmless |

## When to Contact Healthcare (Hvornår kontakte sundhedspersonale)

### Immediately (Ring 112 or go to hospital)

- Baby unresponsive or very difficult to wake
- High-pitched, continuous crying
- Arched back, stiff body
- Fever with jaundice

### Same Day (Contact doctor/jordmoder)

- Jaundice appearing within first 24 hours
- Rapidly increasing jaundice
- Baby not feeding well
- Fewer than expected wet/dirty diapers
- Pale or white stools

### Within 24-48 Hours

- Jaundice still present after 2 weeks
- Any concerns about feeding or alertness

## Key Messages for Parents

1. **Jaundice is common** - affects most newborns to some degree
2. **Usually harmless** - resolves on its own with frequent feeding
3. **Monitor progression** - watch if it spreads below the chest
4. **Feed frequently** - helps baby excrete bilirubin
5. **Know warning signs** - seek help for severe symptoms
6. **Don't stop breastfeeding** - unless specifically advised by doctor
