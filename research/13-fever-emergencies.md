## Critical Information

> **Danish:** "Alle børn under 6 måneder med feber (temperatur >38°) bør ses af en læge."
>
> **English:** "All children under 6 months with fever (temperature >38°C) should be seen by a doctor."
>
> *Source: Sundhedsstyrelsen*

## Fever Thresholds by Age

### Under 3 Months - HIGHEST RISK

| Temperature | Action | Contact |
|-------------|--------|---------|
| >38.0°C (rectal) | **Seek immediate medical attention** | Call 1813 or go to hospital |
| Any fever | Requires evaluation | Contact doctor same day |

> **Danish:** "Et barn under 3 måneder med feber skal altid vurderes af en læge."
>
> **English:** "A child under 3 months with fever must always be evaluated by a doctor."

### 3-6 Months - HIGH RISK

| Temperature | Action | Contact |
|-------------|--------|---------|
| >38.0°C | See a doctor | Contact doctor or call 1813 |
| >38.5°C | Urgent evaluation | Call 1813 or go to hospital |
| With other symptoms | Immediate attention | Call 1813 |

### 6-12 Months

| Temperature | Action | Contact |
|-------------|--------|---------|
| 38.0-38.5°C | Monitor closely | Give fluids, watch for symptoms |
| >38.5°C | Consider medical evaluation | Contact doctor if concerned |
| >39.0°C with symptoms | See doctor | Call 1813 |
| >40.0°C | Urgent | Call 1813 or go to hospital |

### Over 12 Months

| Temperature | Action | Contact |
|-------------|--------|---------|
| 38.0-39.0°C | Monitor at home | Give fluids, fever medicine if needed |
| >39.0°C | Monitor closely | Contact doctor if no improvement |
| >40.0°C | Seek evaluation | Call 1813 |
| With warning signs | Immediate attention | Call 1813 or 112 |

## How to Measure Temperature (Sådan Måler du Temperatur)

### Recommended Methods by Age

| Age | Method | Danish | Notes |
|-----|--------|--------|-------|
| 0-2 years | Rectal | Endetarmen | Most accurate |
| 0-5 years | Ear (alternative) | Øret | Quick, less accurate |
| 3+ years | Armpit (alternative) | Armhulen | Add 0.5°C to reading |

> **Danish:** "Rektal temperaturmåling er den mest præcise metode til små børn."
>
> **English:** "Rectal temperature measurement is the most accurate method for small children."

### Normal Temperature Ranges

| Location | Normal Range |
|----------|--------------|
| Rectal | 36.5-37.9°C |
| Ear | 35.8-38.0°C |
| Armpit | 36.0-37.4°C (add 0.5 for core temp) |

## Emergency Warning Signs (Alarmtegn)

### CALL 112 IMMEDIATELY IF:

| Sign | Danish | Description |
|------|--------|-------------|
| Difficulty breathing | Vejrtrækningsbesvær | Rapid, labored, or noisy breathing |
| Blue lips/tongue | Blå læber/tunge | Cyanosis - lack of oxygen |
| Unresponsive | Reagerer ikke | Won't wake, no response to stimulation |
| Seizure/convulsion | Kramper | Shaking, stiffening, loss of consciousness |
| Rash that doesn't fade | Udslæt der ikke forsvinder ved tryk | Petechiae - possible meningitis |
| Bulging fontanelle | Udbulet fontanelle | With fever - possible meningitis |

> **Danish:** "Ring 112 ved kramper, vejrtrækningsbesvær, eller hvis barnet ikke reagerer."
>
> **English:** "Call 112 for seizures, breathing difficulties, or if the child is unresponsive."

### Call 1813 (Medical Helpline) IF:

| Sign | Danish | When to Call |
|------|--------|--------------|
| Fever under 6 months | Feber under 6 måneder | Any temperature >38°C |
| High fever | Høj feber | >39°C with no improvement |
| Won't drink | Vil ikke drikke | Refusing fluids for several hours |
| Very sleepy | Meget søvnig | Difficult to wake, unusually drowsy |
| Weak or floppy | Slap | Less active than normal |
| Crying inconsolably | Græder utrøsteligt | Cannot be comforted |
| Fewer wet diapers | Færre våde bleer | Signs of dehydration |

## Dehydration Signs (Tegn på Dehydrering)

Dehydration is a serious concern with fever, especially in infants.

### Signs to Watch For

| Sign | Danish | Severity |
|------|--------|----------|
| Dry mouth/lips | Tør mund/læber | Mild |
| Fewer wet diapers | Færre våde bleer | Moderate |
| No tears when crying | Ingen tårer ved gråd | Moderate |
| Sunken fontanelle | Indsunket fontanelle | Moderate-Severe |
| Sunken eyes | Indsunkne øjne | Severe |
| Very sleepy/lethargic | Meget sløv | Severe |
| Cool, mottled skin | Kølig, marmoreret hud | Severe |

### Expected Wet Diapers

| Age | Minimum Wet Diapers/Day |
|-----|-------------------------|
| 0-5 days | See first-week guide |
| After day 5 | 6+ wet diapers |
| If fewer | Contact healthcare provider |

## Meningitis Warning Signs (Meningitis Advarselstegn)

Meningitis is rare but serious. Know these signs:

| Sign | Danish | Description |
|------|--------|-------------|
| High fever | Høj feber | Often sudden onset |
| Stiff neck | Stiv nakke | Won't touch chin to chest |
| Sensitivity to light | Lysfølsomhed | Avoids bright light |
| Severe headache | Svær hovedpine | Infant: high-pitched cry |
| Rash | Udslæt | Petechiae - red/purple spots that don't fade |
| Bulging fontanelle | Udbulet fontanelle | In infants |
| Vomiting | Opkastning | Often projectile |
| Confusion/drowsiness | Forvirring/døsighed | Altered consciousness |

### The Glass Test (Glasprøven)

Press a clear glass against a rash. If the rash **does not fade** when pressed, seek immediate medical attention.

> **Danish:** "Tryk et glas mod udslættet. Hvis det ikke forsvinder ved tryk, ring 112 straks."
>
> **English:** "Press a glass against the rash. If it doesn't fade when pressed, call 112 immediately."

## Febrile Seizures (Feberkramper)

Febrile seizures can occur in children aged 6 months to 5 years with fever.

### What to Do During a Seizure

| Action | Danish |
|--------|--------|
| Stay calm | Bevar roen |
| Place child on side | Læg barnet på siden |
| Clear area of hazards | Fjern farlige genstande |
| Do NOT put anything in mouth | Læg IKKE noget i munden |
| Time the seizure | Tag tid |
| Call 112 if >5 minutes | Ring 112 hvis >5 minutter |

### After the Seizure

- Child may be sleepy/confused - this is normal
- Contact 1813 or doctor
- First-time seizure: always seek medical evaluation

---

## When Fever is NOT an Emergency

In otherwise healthy children over 6 months:

- Fever alone is not dangerous
- Fever is the body fighting infection
- Most fevers are caused by viral infections
- Focus on how the child looks and behaves, not just the number

> **Danish:** "Det vigtigste er ikke temperaturen, men hvordan barnet har det."
>
> **English:** "The most important thing is not the temperature, but how the child is doing."

## Home Care for Fever (Hjemmepleje ved Feber)

### Do's

| Action | Danish |
|--------|--------|
| Offer frequent fluids | Tilbyd hyppigt væske |
| Dress in light clothing | Klæd i let tøj |
| Keep room comfortable | Hold stuen behageligt tempereret |
| Give fever medicine if needed | Giv febermedicin om nødvendigt |
| Monitor for warning signs | Overvåg for advarselstegn |

### Don'ts

| Don't | Danish |
|-------|--------|
| Don't bundle up | Svøb ikke barnet ind |
| Don't use cold baths | Brug ikke kolde bade |
| Don't force eating | Tving ikke barnet til at spise |
| Don't give aspirin (under 16) | Giv ikke aspirin (under 16 år) |

### Fever Medicine (Febermedicin)

| Medicine | Danish | Age |
|----------|--------|-----|
| Paracetamol | Paracetamol (Panodil, Calpol) | From 3 months |
| Ibuprofen | Ibuprofen (Ipren, Nurofen) | From 6 months |

**Important:** Follow dosage instructions based on weight, not age.

## Key Messages for Parents

1. **Under 6 months with fever = always contact doctor**
2. **Trust your instincts** - if worried, seek help
3. **Watch behavior, not just temperature** - how the child acts matters most
4. **Know emergency signs** - breathing problems, unresponsiveness, rash
5. **Stay calm** - most fevers are not dangerous
6. **Keep hydrated** - offer fluids frequently
