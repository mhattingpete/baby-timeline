## Key Guidelines for Infants

### Non-Mobile Infants (Babies Who Cannot Crawl/Walk)

> **Danish:** "Spædbørn, som ikke kan kravle eller gå, skal holdes væk fra direkte sollys, og behøver derfor ikke bruge solcreme."
>
> **English:** "Infants who cannot crawl or walk should be kept away from direct sunlight, and therefore do not need sunscreen."
>
> *Source: Sundhedsstyrelsen*

| Guideline | Danish | Details |
|-----------|--------|---------|
| Avoid direct sunlight | Undgå direkte sollys | Keep in shade at all times |
| No sunscreen needed | Ingen solcreme nødvendig | If kept in shade |
| Protective clothing | Beskyttende tøj | Cover skin when outside |
| Brimmed hat | Hat med skygge | Protect face and neck |

## Age-Specific Recommendations

### 0-6 Months

| Protection | Details |
|------------|---------|
| Primary strategy | Keep out of direct sun completely |
| Sunscreen | Generally not recommended for this age |
| Clothing | Long sleeves, pants, hat |
| Best practice | Stay in shade, limit time outside when UV is high |

### 6-12 Months

| Protection | Details |
|------------|---------|
| Primary strategy | Shade + clothing + limited sunscreen |
| Sunscreen | Can use on exposed areas (SPF 30+, broad spectrum) |
| Clothing | UPF-rated clothing ideal |
| Hat | Wide brim covering face, ears, neck |

### 1+ Years

| Protection | Details |
|------------|---------|
| Full sun protection plan | Shade, clothing, sunscreen, timing |
| Sunscreen | SPF 30+ on all exposed skin |
| Reapply | Every 2 hours, after swimming/sweating |

## UV Index and Timing

### When Protection is Needed

> **Danish:** "Beskyt huden når UV-indeks er 3 eller derover."
>
> **English:** "Protect skin when UV index is 3 or above."

| UV Index | Risk Level | Danish | Action |
|----------|------------|--------|--------|
| 1-2 | Low | Lav | Minimal protection needed |
| 3-5 | Moderate | Moderat | Protection recommended |
| 6-7 | High | Høj | Protection required |
| 8-10 | Very High | Meget høj | Extra protection required |
| 11+ | Extreme | Ekstrem | Avoid sun exposure |

### Typical High UV Times in Denmark

| Time Period | UV Level |
|-------------|----------|
| April-September | UV can reach 3+ |
| 12:00-15:00 | Highest UV (up to 6-7 in summer) |
| Cloudy days | UV can still be significant |

> **Danish:** "I Danmark er UV-indekset typisk 3 eller mere fra april til september, især mellem kl. 12 og 15."
>
> **English:** "In Denmark, the UV index is typically 3 or more from April to September, especially between 12:00 and 15:00."

## Protective Measures (Beskyttelsesforanstaltninger)

### 1. Shade (Skygge)

| Method | Danish | Notes |
|--------|--------|-------|
| Parasol/umbrella | Parasol/paraply | Position to block sun |
| Canopy/tent | Baldakin/telt | For beach/park |
| Trees | Træer | Natural shade |
| Stroller canopy | Klapvognskaleche | Ensure good coverage |
| Time inside | Tid indenfor | During peak UV hours |

**Note:** Shade reduces but doesn't eliminate UV exposure.

### 2. Clothing (Tøj)

| Feature | Danish | Recommendation |
|---------|--------|----------------|
| Long sleeves | Lange ærmer | Cover arms |
| Long pants | Lange bukser | Cover legs |
| Tightly woven | Tætvævet | Better protection |
| Dark colors | Mørke farver | Block more UV |
| UPF-rated | UPF-mærket | Best protection |

> **Danish:** "Tætvævet, mørkt tøj giver bedre beskyttelse end løst, lyst tøj."
>
> **English:** "Tightly woven, dark clothing provides better protection than loose, light clothing."

### 3. Hats (Hatte)

| Type | Danish | Protection |
|------|--------|------------|
| Wide-brim (best) | Bred skygge | Face, ears, neck |
| Legionnaire style | Legionærstil | Face, ears, neck |
| Cap with flap | Kasket med nakkeflap | Face and neck |
| Regular cap | Almindelig kasket | Face only (less ideal) |

### 4. Sunglasses (Solbriller)

| Feature | Recommendation |
|---------|----------------|
| UV protection | 100% UVA and UVB |
| Style | Wraparound for babies |
| Fit | Secure on baby's face |
| Age | Appropriate when baby tolerates |

## Sunscreen Guidelines (Solcreme Retningslinjer)

### When to Use

| Age | Sunscreen Use |
|-----|---------------|
| Under 6 months | Generally avoid; keep baby in shade |
| 6+ months | Can use on small exposed areas |
| All ages | Prefer shade and clothing first |

### Choosing Sunscreen

| Feature | Recommendation | Danish |
|---------|----------------|--------|
| SPF | 30 or higher | SPF 30 eller højere |
| Spectrum | Broad spectrum (UVA + UVB) | Bredspektret |
| Type | Physical/mineral (zinc oxide, titanium dioxide) | Fysisk/mineralsk |
| Fragrance | Fragrance-free | Parfumefri |
| For sensitive skin | Hypoallergenic | Hypoallergen |

### Ingredients to Avoid (Under Age 12)

> **Danish:** "Solcreme med stoffet 4-MBC (4-methylbenzylidene camphor) er mistænkt for at være hormonforstyrrende og bør ikke anvendes til børn under 12 år."
>
> **English:** "Sunscreen with the substance 4-MBC (4-methylbenzylidene camphor) is suspected of being hormone-disrupting and should not be used for children under 12 years."
>
> *Source: Sundhedsstyrelsen*

| Avoid | Danish | Reason |
|-------|--------|--------|
| 4-MBC | 4-MBC | Potential hormone disruptor |
| Oxybenzone | Oxybenzon | Potential allergen/hormone disruptor |
| High fragrances | Stærk parfume | Skin irritation |

### How to Apply

| Step | Details |
|------|---------|
| Amount | Generous layer |
| Timing | 15-30 minutes before sun exposure |
| Areas | All exposed skin (don't forget ears, feet) |
| Reapply | Every 2 hours |
| After water | Reapply after swimming/drying off |

## Stroller and Car Safety

### Stroller Covers

| Warning | Danish |
|---------|--------|
| Don't cover stroller with blanket | Dæk ikke klapvognen med tæppe |
| Reduces airflow | Reducerer luftcirkulation |
| Can cause overheating | Kan forårsage overophedning |
| Temperature inside can rise significantly | Temperaturen indeni kan stige betydeligt |

**Better alternatives:**
- Use built-in canopy
- Attach clip-on parasol
- Find shade
- Stay indoors during peak UV

### Car Windows

| Fact | Details |
|------|---------|
| Front windshield | Blocks most UVA and UVB |
| Side/back windows | Block UVB but not UVA |
| Solution | Window shades, sunshade for baby |

## Signs of Sunburn and Heat Issues

### Sunburn Signs (Solskoldning)

| Sign | Danish | Action |
|------|--------|--------|
| Red skin | Rød hud | Get out of sun, cool skin |
| Warm to touch | Varm ved berøring | Apply cool compress |
| Fussiness | Urolig | Monitor, comfort |
| Blisters | Blærer | Seek medical attention |

### Heat Exhaustion Signs

| Sign | Danish | Action |
|------|--------|--------|
| Excessive sweating | Kraftig sved | Cool down, fluids |
| Fussiness, crying | Uro, gråd | Get to cool place |
| Flushed skin | Rødmende hud | Remove layers |
| Lethargy | Sløvhed | Seek medical attention |
| Hot, dry skin | Varm, tør hud | **Emergency - call for help** |

## Special Considerations

### Medication and Sun Sensitivity

Some medications can increase sun sensitivity. Check with doctor if baby is on:
- Antibiotics
- Antihistamines
- Other medications

### Reflective Surfaces

UV is stronger near:
- Water (reflects UV)
- Sand (reflects UV)
- Concrete (reflects UV)
- Snow (reflects UV)

Extra protection needed in these environments.

### Vitamin D Balance

While sun protection is important, vitamin D is also needed:

> **Danish:** "Alle børn fra 2 uger til 4 år skal have D-vitamin tilskud."
>
> **English:** "All children from 2 weeks to 4 years should have vitamin D supplements."

This ensures vitamin D needs are met without sun exposure risks.

## Key Messages for Parents

1. **Infants in shade** - Keep non-mobile babies out of direct sun completely
2. **No blanket over stroller** - Causes overheating, use proper shade
3. **Clothing first** - Long sleeves, pants, and wide-brimmed hat
4. **Sunscreen secondary** - Use when clothing/shade not possible (6+ months)
5. **Check UV index** - Protect when UV is 3 or higher
6. **Avoid peak hours** - 12:00-15:00 has highest UV
7. **Still give D-vitamin** - Supplements ensure vitamin D without sun risk
8. **Watch for signs** - Sunburn, overheating require action
